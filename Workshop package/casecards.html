Loss of Control

A grieving customer asked Air Canada's support chatbot about bereavement fares. The chatbot invented a discount policy that didn't exist, advising the customer they could apply for a reduced fare after purchase. The customer booked based on that advice.

Air Canada argued in court that it wasn't responsible for what its chatbot said. The court ruled against them. First decision of its kind: your AI is your agent. No disclaimer absolves you.

Guardrail Failure · DPD · UK · 2024 · Logistics
"Your brand.
Your channel. No override."
Guardrail Failure

A customer convinced DPD's branded support chatbot to write a poem criticising the company. The chatbot complied without hesitation. Screenshots went viral on social media within hours.

DPD's name was on the channel. DPD's reputation took the hit. No one at DPD had defined what the AI was and wasn't allowed to say. The customer got what they wanted. DPD did not.

Regulatory Exposure

The official branding implied an accuracy the model couldn't deliver. Businesses made real operational decisions based on the advice before the retraction was issued.

Misaligned Objective · Klarna · 2024 · Fintech
"The AI did exactly
what it was told."
Wrong Objective

Klarna replaced a significant portion of its customer support workforce with AI, optimising the system for ticket resolution speed and cost reduction. The AI performed exactly as designed. Metrics improved.

Customer satisfaction scores dropped. The failure was not a loss of control - no one asked the AI to care about the customer experience. The AI succeeded at its objective. That was the problem.
