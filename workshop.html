The Framework

Score before you build.
Plot before you commit.

The GenAI Triage Workshop is built around a two-axis prioritisation matrix developed for leadership teams. Every GenAI initiative your organisation is considering gets scored against three risk factors, then plotted on the matrix. The quadrant it lands in tells you the right posture.

The three scoring factors: the customer's emotional state at the point of contact, your audience's actual AI tolerance, and your compliance burden. Score first. Plot after. The order matters.

The framework was built before the consumer data came in. The research calibrates the scores to what your customers actually expect — not what vendors assume they will accept.

The Session

What happens
in the room.

01
Pre-session preparation
You bring a list of GenAI initiatives your organisation is actively considering or already running. No preparation required beyond that. The workshop works with what you have.
02
Score each initiative
Working through each initiative together, you run it through the three-factor Risk Scorecard. This is where the hard conversations happen and where hidden assumptions surface. Facilitated, structured, and time-boxed.
03
Plot the matrix
Each initiative gets plotted based on its score. Patterns emerge. Some ideas move to Quick Wins. Some land in the Danger Zone. A few become Strategic Bets with a clear pilot path.
04
Leave with a roadmap
A prioritised, scored matrix with your actual initiatives plotted. A shared language your legal, compliance, and executive stakeholders can engage with. And a clear next step for each idea.

Common Questions

Honest answers
to the real objections.

"We already have an AI strategy."
Most organisations have a direction, not a triage model. The workshop doesn't replace your strategy. It gives your team a shared scoring language to test your current initiatives against real customer-centric risk before committing resources. If your strategy is solid, the matrix will confirm it. If there are gaps, better to find them in the room than in production.
"Our compliance team won't move on GenAI right now."
That is exactly why the workshop exists. The Risk Scorecard gives compliance a structured input method instead of a binary yes or no on a finished proposal. Most teams find that compliance is more willing to engage when they are part of the scoring conversation rather than being handed a decision to approve.
"We're not ready yet."
The workshop works best before you are fully committed to a direction, not after. If your organisation is in the "we need to figure out where to start" phase, the matrix is the right tool. It helps you identify the Quick Wins you can move on immediately and the Strategic Bets worth planning for, before anyone has written a line of code or signed a vendor contract.
